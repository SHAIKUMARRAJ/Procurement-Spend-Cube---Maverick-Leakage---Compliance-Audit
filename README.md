# Procurement-Spend-Cube---Maverick-Leakage---Compliance-Audit

📋 Project Overview
This project represents a comprehensive, data-driven audit of an enterprise procurement pipeline totaling $446.52 Million in transaction volume. The core objective of this analysis was to map macro corporate spend patterns, measure transactional volatility, and isolate non-compliant financial leakage across three major operational categories: Hardware, Software, and Services.
By evaluating data relationships across the transactional lifecycle, this audit successfully verified a highly disciplined core purchasing structure while identifying $1.09 Million in maverick spend risk that requires targeted process controls.

🔬 Core Audit Modules & Methodologies

1. Spend Distribution & Volatility Profiling
Using descriptive statistical modeling, the project profiled the behavioral DNA of each purchasing category by measuring the relationship between standard transactional averages and their variance (Standard Deviation):
•	Hardware & Software: Exhibited stable, highly predictable procurement profiles. Day-to-day transactions remain tightly clustered around the mean, signaling mature purchasing frameworks and standard corporate renewal cycles.
•	Services: Highlighted extreme structural volatility. In this category, the standard deviation significantly outpaced the transactional average, driven by a massive single-transaction ceiling of $500,000. This mathematically proves that service agreements lack standardized pricing tiers and represent the highest volatility risk in operations.

🔑 Key Strategic Takeaways
•	World-Class Process Discipline: The organization maintains an exceptional macro compliance profile, with 99.76% of all spend moving through fully authorized channels. Hardware stands as the absolute gold standard with a flawless 0.00% leakage rate.
•	Services as the Primary Bottleneck: Services represent 72% of all maverick spend leakage, totaling over $785K in unapproved or blocked transactions. This is where the organization experiences the greatest operational friction and delayed contract commitments.
•	The Scale Reality: While a $1.09 Million maverick pipeline is a substantial dollar figure requiring intervention, contrasting it against the $445 Million approved base proves to leadership that corporate purchasing controls are fundamentally robust, meaning targeted—rather than sweeping—remediations are required.

💡 Strategic Action Plan & Recommendations

1.	Enforce Master Service Agreements (MSAs): Transition services away from unpredictable, ad-hoc purchase orders into fixed-rate MSAs. Pre-negotiating tier structures will immediately flatten the extreme volatility and price spikes identified in the distribution audit.
2.	Implement Threshold-Based Approval Tiering: Maintain low-friction, automated approvals for stable, uniform categories like Hardware and Software. For Services, introduce a multi-stage approval signature chain that scales based on the dollar size of the request.
3.	Automate Upstream Data Validation: Embed mandatory category fields into the initial purchase requisition portal. Forcing proper data classification at the point of entry ensures that zero transactions bypass standard compliance definitions before touching the general ledger.
